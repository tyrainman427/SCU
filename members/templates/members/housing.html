HOUSING ASSISTANCE PROGRAM




    • Temporary affordable housing for low income members (As long as SCU has available units)
      • Members will receive training on how to save money, building equity through investments and how to live within their means to decrease financial insecurities.
      • Members will be allowed to apply for temporary housing under certain guidelines. Tenant selection and occupancy policies permit SCU to examine an applicant’s history, including criminal background, to ensure selection of a responsible tenant. SCU has the power to deny admission to individuals with a history of use or abuse of drugs or alcohol, or of criminal behavior that interferes with the peaceful enjoyment of the premises by other residents. Eligibility for assistance under SCU programs are limited to U.S. citizens who are members of SCU. SCU Program participants are also required to submit household income documentation each year to determine continued eligibility in the program. This process is called the annual review. Tenant understands that the lease renewal process cannot exceed a period of 3 years unless an extension is granted by SCU’s board of directors. SCU will provide temporary affordable housing to SCU members who meet the minimum requirements on a first come first serve basis as long as housing is available and tenants are committed to achieving the one or more of the following goals:
        • GED or High School Diploma
        • Training classes / programs
        • Getting a CT Driver’s License
        • Budgeting
        • Becoming debt free
        • Home Buyer Education
        • Attend college for a certificate or degree
        • Purchasing a car
        • Repair or build a good credit score
        • Saving Money (proof of an untouchable account for the term of the lease must be provided and tent must be able to afford to put away $50 a paycheck in such account in addition to paying $300 a month for rent and utilities)
        • Buying a House
